Drivers of customer churn in Q3 2025

Five-section analysis of customer behaviour and churn outcomes. Sourced from customers_q3.csv (12,450 customers).

i

Cohort overview & churn baseline

accepted

Of 12,450 customers active during Q3 2025, 1,780 churned — a baseline rate of 14.3%.1 Churn was unevenly distributed across signup cohorts: customers acquired in late 2024 churned at 21.4%, almost twice the rate of customers acquired before mid-2023.2

# Compute churn rate and 95% CI by plan tier import pandas as pd from scipy import stats df = pd.read_csv("customers_q3.csv") tier_churn = (df.groupby("plan_tier")["churned"] .agg(["mean", "count", "sum"])) tier_churn["ci_low"], tier_churn["ci_high"] = wilson_ci( tier_churn["sum"], tier_churn["count"]) chi2, p = stats.chi2_contingency(pd.crosstab(df.plan_tier, df.churned))[:2]

Proposed interpretation

Churn rate descends monotonically with plan tier: Basic customers churned at 18.4%, more than four times the Enterprise rate of 4.2%. The contingency test confirms the association is unlikely to be chance (χ² = 287.4, p < 0.001).3 Tier alone, however, conflates several confounds — Enterprise customers are also longer-tenured and more engaged — so the multivariate model in §5 will revisit this.
